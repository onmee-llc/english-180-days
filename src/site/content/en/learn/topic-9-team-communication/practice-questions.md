## How to use this module

For each question:
1. **First pass:** Answer out loud with notes allowed.
2. **Second pass:** Answer out loud — no notes, no preparation.
3. **Record yourself** on at least one question per session and listen back.

**Target:** Each answer should feel automatic — natural, structured, confident.

## Scoring yourself

After each answer, ask:
- Did my answer show communication skill, not just technical knowledge?
- Did I give a specific example from my experience?
- Did I demonstrate awareness of remote/async work dynamics?
- Did the answer take 2–4 minutes?

## Question 1 — How do you approach code reviews?

**What the interviewer expects:** Both giving and receiving. Constructive, specific, kind. Focus on correctness, readability, edge cases. Show you value the process.

**Structure your answer:** Your philosophy (collaborative, not gatekeeping) → What you look for (correctness, readability, edge cases) → How you give feedback (specific, questions over commands) → How you receive feedback → Real example.

## Question 2 — How do you communicate technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders?

**What the interviewer expects:** Translation skill. Simplify without dumbing down. Use analogies, focus on impact and timeline, avoid jargon.

**Structure your answer:** Understand the audience → Focus on impact (not implementation) → Use simple language → Provide timeline and risks → Real example.

## Question 3 — How do you handle working across time zones in a remote team?

**What the interviewer expects:** Async-first mindset. Clear documentation, overlapping hours strategy, effective handoffs, self-sufficiency.

**Structure your answer:** Async-first principle → Documentation practices → Overlap hours for sync → Clear handoffs → Self-sufficiency → Real example.

## Question 4 — Describe a time you disagreed with a teammate on a technical approach

**What the interviewer expects:** Maturity. You listened, understood their perspective, presented your evidence, and found a resolution. No ego battles.

**Structure your answer:** The disagreement (S) → Both perspectives (T) → How you resolved it (A) → The outcome and what you learned (R).

## Question 5 — How do you onboard yourself to a new codebase quickly?

**What the interviewer expects:** Self-directed learning. Read docs, explore the code, set up locally, find a small task, ask targeted questions. Show you don't need hand-holding.

**Structure your answer:** Documentation first → Local setup → Read recent PRs → Pick a small task → Ask targeted questions → Contribute within first week → Real example.
