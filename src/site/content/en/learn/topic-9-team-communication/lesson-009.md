## Session goal

Work through a realistic conflict scenario: misaligned expectations, async communication.

## Shadowing passage

> I want to tell you about a situation I handled on a remote team last year, because I think it illustrates how I approach conflict. I had a teammate who consistently merged PRs without waiting for my review, even on components I owned. In a co-located team you can walk over and have a conversation. Remote, I had to be more deliberate. First, I did not react in the public Slack channel. Instead, I sent a direct message asking if we could do a quick sync. In the call, I opened by acknowledging the pressure we were both under: 'I know we are both trying to move fast.' Then I explained the specific impact: 'When PRs get merged before I review them, I lose visibility into changes to the auth layer, which is the component I am responsible for. Last week it led to an incident.' Then I proposed a solution: 'Could we agree that any PR touching the auth or payment layers gets at least one review from me before merge? Even if it is a quick one?' They agreed immediately — they had not realized the impact. The conflict was not about personality. It was about unclear ownership and implicit expectations. Making those explicit resolved it.

## Key phrases

| Phrase | IPA | Note |

| implicit expectations | /ɪmˈplɪsɪt ɪkˌspɛkˈteɪʃənz/ | unwritten rules — the source of most remote team friction |

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Think of a real conflict you had. Did you address it directly, or hope it would resolve itself?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
