## Session goal

Practice the 'new engineer on the team' first communication — humble, curious, credible.

## Shadowing passage

> Hi everyone — I am Robert, and I am excited to be joining the team. A bit of background on me: I have been a backend engineer for about twelve years, with a focus on distributed systems and, more recently, AI integration. Before joining you I spent three years building an AI-powered content automation platform, which means I am comfortable with both the infrastructure and the LLM integration sides of what I hear you are working on. In terms of how I like to work: I prefer async communication by default and try to keep my calendar free for deep work. I am happy to review code, pair program, and engage in architecture discussions. I do not like meetings without a clear agenda. In my first two weeks I am planning to read — not to ship features. I want to understand the codebase, the deployment process, and the existing architectural decisions before I start suggesting changes. I will have questions, probably a lot of them, and I would really appreciate knowing who I should direct them to for each area. Looking forward to working with all of you.

## Key phrases

| Phrase | IPA | Note |
|--------|-----|------|
| comfortable with both sides | /ˈkʌmfərtəbl wɪð boʊθ saɪdz/ | signals breadth — you are not siloed |
| deep work | /diːp wɜːrk/ | Cal Newport term — focused, uninterrupted technical work |
| read, not ship | /riːd nɒt ʃɪp/ | strong first 2 weeks posture — understand before changing |

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Does your intro strike the balance between confidence and humility? Record it and check.
