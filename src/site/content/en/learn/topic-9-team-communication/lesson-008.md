## Session goal

Practice Socratic mentoring: ask questions that lead to the answer rather than giving it.

## Shadowing passage

> When I am mentoring someone who is stuck, my goal is to help them think through the problem rather than hand them the answer. Here is how that usually sounds in practice. They come to me and say: 'I cannot figure out why this test is failing.' Instead of looking at the code immediately, I ask: 'What does the error message actually say?' Often they have not read it carefully. Then: 'What did you expect to happen, and what actually happened?' This forces them to articulate the gap. Then: 'What is the simplest thing you can add to understand what the actual state of the system is at the point of failure?' — usually a log or a print statement. What I am trying to do is teach the debugging process, not fix this one test. The short-term cost is that it takes longer than just giving the answer. The long-term benefit is that they do not come back with the same class of problem twice. The most common mistake I see in mentors is impatience — they fix the problem themselves because it is faster, and they deprive the junior engineer of the learning experience.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
