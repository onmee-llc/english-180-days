## Session goal

Practice the format of a high-quality async update that does not create follow-up questions.

## Shadowing passage

> Here is a model Slack message I would send to update a stakeholder on a blocked task. 'Hey Sarah — quick update on the payment retry service. I have completed the implementation and the unit tests are passing. I am blocked on completing end-to-end testing because the staging payment provider account is returning 403 errors. I reached out to the vendor at 2pm today and have a ticket open with them — reference number is P-48291. My best estimate: if they respond by tomorrow morning, I can complete testing and open the PR by Thursday EOD. If not, I may need to push to Friday. Do you want me to escalate to the support tier above, or should I wait for their response? No action needed from you right now — just wanted to keep you informed and give you the chance to unblock me faster if you have a contact there.' Notice what this message does: it states progress, states the blocker specifically, says what I have already done about it, gives a time estimate, and asks a specific yes or no question. It does not require the reader to ask any follow-up questions to understand the situation.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Does your written communication eliminate follow-up questions, or generate them?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
