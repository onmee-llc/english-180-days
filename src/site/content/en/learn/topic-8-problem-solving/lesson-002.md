## Session goal

Describe your debugging methodology clearly enough that a junior engineer could follow it.

## Shadowing passage

> My debugging process has four stages and I follow the same order every time. First: isolate. I narrow down which component owns the problem before I touch any code. Is it the frontend, the API layer, the backend service, the database, or an external dependency? I use logging and metrics to localize the fault, not intuition. Second: reproduce. I need to be able to trigger the bug reliably before I try to fix it. If I cannot reproduce it, I am guessing. I write a minimal test case or find the exact conditions under which it happens. Third: hypothesize. Once I can reproduce it, I form a specific hypothesis — 'I think the variable X is null when Y happens' — and I make the hypothesis falsifiable. Fourth: test and fix. I change exactly one thing at a time. I verify that the change fixes the symptom without introducing a new problem. Then I document what I found and add a test to prevent regression. The most common mistake I see is skipping straight to 'fix' without finishing 'isolate' and 'reproduce.'

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Anti-Translation Drill — Interview Mode *(5 min)*

Someone just asked you the Reflection question in a **real interview**. Answer now.

- You have **60 seconds**. No pauses longer than 3 seconds.
- Fill gaps with: *"Let me think about that…"* / *"The way I see it…"* / *"To give you a concrete example…"*
- **Record this answer** separately from your shadowing recording.
- Play it back. Did you sound like someone they'd hire?

> Goal: Simulate interview pressure. The ability to speak under pressure without translating is what separates "good English" from "interview-ready English".
