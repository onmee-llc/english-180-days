## Session goal

Practice the language of structured exploration when the answer isn't obvious.

## Shadowing passage

> I want to be honest here: I have not worked with this specific system before, so I cannot give you a definitive answer right now. What I can do is walk you through how I would approach it. The first thing is to understand the failure mode. What does the error look like? Is it deterministic — does it always happen under the same conditions — or intermittent? Intermittent failures are almost always related to timing, concurrency, or external dependencies. Deterministic failures are almost always configuration, data, or logic errors. Once I know the failure mode, I would look at the observable signals: logs, metrics, traces. I would look for what changed recently. Most production incidents are caused by something that changed, not by a system suddenly misbehaving without a trigger. Then I would start forming hypotheses ranked by probability, based on pattern recognition from similar systems I have seen. I may not recognize the specific technology, but the failure patterns in distributed systems are actually quite consistent across stacks. I would lean on that. I am confident I could contribute meaningfully to debugging this within an hour of access to the system.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Does your answer sound confident, or apologetic? Aim for honest AND confident.


## Anti-Translation Drill — Interview Mode *(5 min)*

Someone just asked you the Reflection question in a **real interview**. Answer now.

- You have **60 seconds**. No pauses longer than 3 seconds.
- Fill gaps with: *"Let me think about that…"* / *"The way I see it…"* / *"To give you a concrete example…"*
- **Record this answer** separately from your shadowing recording.
- Play it back. Did you sound like someone they'd hire?

> Goal: Simulate interview pressure. The ability to speak under pressure without translating is what separates "good English" from "interview-ready English".
