## Session goal

Practice the language of moving forward under uncertainty without sounding reckless.

## Shadowing passage

> In real engineering work, you almost never have all the information you want before making a decision. The choices are: wait for more information, make a decision with what you have and document your assumptions, or explicitly request the blocking information and define a deadline. My default is the middle option: move forward, document assumptions, make the decision reversible where possible. Here is what that looks like in practice. If I am not sure what the peak load will be for a new service, I will design for a reasonable estimate — say, five hundred requests per second — and I will note explicitly in the architecture document: 'This estimate assumes X. If actual load exceeds Y, we will need to revisit the caching strategy.' That way, when the real number comes in, we know exactly which decisions to revisit and which to leave alone. The antipattern I try to avoid is paralysis — waiting for perfect information that will never arrive. Ship something, measure it, iterate. That is how real engineering gets done.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Think of a recent decision you made with incomplete information. How did you document your assumptions?
