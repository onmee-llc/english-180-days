## Session goal

Practice the language of assessing technical health without being dismissive.

## Shadowing passage

> When I join a new team or take ownership of an existing system, I follow a consistent assessment process before making any changes. First week: read, do not touch. I look at the README, the deployment pipeline, the test coverage report, and the most recent incident post-mortems. That gives me a picture of the system's health and the team's engineering culture. Second week: map the critical paths. Which flows are business-critical? Where do failures have the most impact? I want to understand the system's blast radius before I risk anything. Third week: identify the top three risks. Not the messiest code, not the most annoying technical debt — the three things most likely to cause a production incident in the next six months. I write a short summary and share it with the team, framed as observations rather than criticisms. The goal is not to judge the previous engineers — systems always reflect the constraints under which they were built. The goal is to improve reliability going forward. Then I prioritize: quick wins that build trust, versus hard structural improvements that require planning.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

If you were handed your current codebase to evaluate, what would you put in your top three risks?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Interview Mode *(5 min)*

Someone just asked you the Reflection question in a **real interview**. Answer now.

- You have **60 seconds**. No pauses longer than 3 seconds.
- Fill gaps with: *"Let me think about that…"* / *"The way I see it…"* / *"To give you a concrete example…"*
- **Record this answer** separately from your shadowing recording.
- Play it back. Did you sound like someone they'd hire?

> Goal: Simulate interview pressure. The ability to speak under pressure without translating is what separates "good English" from "interview-ready English".
