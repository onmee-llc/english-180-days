## Part 1 — Skim (5 minutes)

Abstract articles use fewer diagrams and more argument structure. Look for:
- The opening thesis (often the first or last sentence of the introduction)
- Section headers that signal the logical flow
- The final section — often a "what still needs to be solved"

After skimming, answer:

**Starter vocabulary:**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| alignment | the property of an AI system reliably pursuing the goals humans intend |
| misalignment | when an AI system pursues objectives that diverge from human intentions, even inadvertently |
| corrigibility | the willingness of an AI system to be corrected, modified, or shut down by humans |
| value learning | the task of inferring human values from behavior, preferences, and feedback |
| interpretability | research into understanding what is happening inside a model's computation |
| emergent behavior | capabilities or behaviors that appear in a model that were not explicitly trained for |
| scalable oversight | techniques to maintain meaningful human supervision as AI capabilities grow |
