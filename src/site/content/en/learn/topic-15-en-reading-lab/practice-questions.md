## Questions to ask about every article

After each Reading Lab session, challenge yourself with these questions. Answer each one out loud in English.

### Comprehension

1. What problem does this article address?
2. What solution or approach does the author propose or explain?
3. What evidence or examples does the author use to support the main point?
4. What does the author assume the reader already knows?
5. What is left unresolved or mentioned as a limitation?

### Vocabulary application

6. Use 3 of the new vocabulary words you extracted in a sentence about your own project.
7. If you had to explain the key concept to a junior engineer, how would you say it? (30 seconds)
8. Is there any vocabulary in this article that is used differently from how you've seen it before?

### Summary and synthesis

9. Write your one-sentence summary. Say it aloud. Is it under 30 words?
10. How does this article connect to your KPI 1 or KPI 2 work?
11. Would you share this article with your team? What would you write in the Slack message?
12. If you had to present the main idea of this article in 2 minutes at a meeting, what would you say?

---

## Self-assessment for one-sentence summaries

Rate each summary you write using these criteria:

| Criterion | Good | Needs work |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Under 30 words | ✓ | Over 30 words |
| Contains the article's main claim | ✓ | Just describes the topic, not the argument |
| Grammatically correct English | ✓ | Grammar errors or unnatural phrasing |
| No Vietnamese words | ✓ | Vietnamese word slipped in |
