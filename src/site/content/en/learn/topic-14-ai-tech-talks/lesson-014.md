## Shadowing passage — pre-talk confidence routine

> Thirty minutes before a technical talk, I do three things. First, I stand up — not sit — and say my opening hook out loud three times. Standing activates a different energy than sitting at a desk. Second, I look at my three take-away points for the talk and say each one as a single sentence, as if explaining to someone who just walked in. If I can't say each point in one clear sentence, I'm not ready. Third, I remind myself: the audience wants me to succeed. They're not waiting for me to fail — they came to learn something. Most technical presentations go well not because the presenter is perfect, but because the audience is generous. The job is to show up prepared, speak clearly, and be honest about what you know and what you don't. After that, the rest takes care of itself. The final thing I do: I leave 3 minutes of blank space at the end of my timer. A talk that runs exactly to time feels rushed. A talk that finishes 3 minutes early and opens for Q&A feels confident.
