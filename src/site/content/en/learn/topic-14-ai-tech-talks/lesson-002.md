## Session goal

Write and deliver three different opening hooks for your Tech Talk #1. Choose the strongest one.

## Shadowing passage — three hook models

**Hook type 1: The surprising number**
> Sixty percent of the engineering time on our AI feature was not spent on the AI. It was spent on retry logic, token management, and monitoring. If you came here today expecting to learn about prompts, I want to reframe that: the hard problem in LLM engineering is not the model. It's the infrastructure around it.

**Hook type 2: The audience's pain point**
> Raise your hand if you've ever had an LLM feature work perfectly in development and silently fail in production with no useful error message. This is almost universal — and it's entirely preventable. Today I'm going to show you the three patterns that eliminate ninety percent of those silent failures.

**Hook type 3: The concrete story**
> Three months ago, we deployed an AI feature that worked flawlessly in staging. On day two of production, costs were five times our estimate, error rate was twelve percent, and we had zero visibility into why. That incident is the reason I'm standing here today.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–10** — Shadow all three hook models twice each.
**Minutes 11–20** — Write your own version of each hook type for your Tech Talk #1.
**Minutes 21–28** — Deliver all three aloud. Record them back to back.
**Minutes 29–30** — Pick the strongest one. Write it on a sticky note or in your notes.

## Reflection

Which hook lands best for your audience — other engineers on your team? Why?

## Anti-Translation Drill

Deliver your chosen hook from memory. No notes. Time yourself — under 60 seconds.
