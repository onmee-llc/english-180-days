## Session goal

Deliver two versions of your system overview: one for engineers (5 minutes, full technical detail), one for non-engineers (2 minutes, outcome and confidence).

## Shadowing passage — Non-technical version

> I built a system that lets engineers ask questions in plain English and get precise answers from our internal documentation — instantly, without searching through wikis or asking colleagues. You type your question, hit enter, and within five seconds you get a specific, sourced answer. The system has been running for three months with over three thousand queries answered correctly eight times out of ten. It costs less than two hundred dollars a month to run. The main benefit isn't speed — it's that junior engineers can now resolve blockers independently that used to require a senior to help. We've seen a noticeable drop in Slack interruptions on the platform channel since we deployed it. The system is ready for the next phase: I'm planning to expand it to cover our runbooks and incident history, so on-call engineers can query it during incidents instead of searching documentation under pressure.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Notice how it focuses on outcomes, not implementation.

**Minutes 6–12** — Shadow aloud 4 times.

**Minutes 13–20** — Replace with your KPI 1 details. Write the non-technical version of your system overview.

**Minutes 21–28** — Record both versions: technical (5 min) and non-technical (2 min). Back to back.

**Minutes 29–30** — Listen back. Which version sounds more confident?
