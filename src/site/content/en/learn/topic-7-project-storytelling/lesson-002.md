## Session goal

Walk through the impact metrics for your three biggest technical projects.

## Shadowing passage

> Every technical achievement needs a number. The question is: what number matters to the business, not just to engineering? For the content automation platform, I frame impact in three ways. Business impact: reduced quarterly planning time from forty hours to twenty minutes per account. That is a hundred-and-nineteen-to-one efficiency ratio. At billing rates, this is approximately three thousand dollars of labor time recovered per account per quarter. At twenty-eight accounts, that is eighty-four thousand dollars annually — for a system that cost about forty thousand dollars to build. Engineering impact: the pipeline processes a complete quarterly plan in twelve to eighteen minutes end-to-end with ninety-two percent automation rate — eight percent of outputs require human editing. Reliability impact: ninety-nine point four percent uptime over the system's first twelve months of production operation. Zero data loss incidents. The discipline for building these numbers: log the before-state before you start. Organizations often don't measure the baseline, so when the system is complete, there's no comparison point. I make a habit of timing the current manual process and documenting it before I write a line of code. When I don't have hard numbers, I state my estimate clearly: 'the team estimated approximately forty hours' — then the result is 'down to twenty minutes, verified by the platform's processing logs.'

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
