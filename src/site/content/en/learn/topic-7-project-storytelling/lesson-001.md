## Session goal

Tell the content automation SaaS story using STAR in 90 seconds.

## Shadowing passage

> Let me use the STAR method to tell the story of the content automation platform. Situation: I was consulting with Pinterest content businesses — agencies and SaaS tools — who spent enormous amounts of time on quarterly content planning. A mid-sized agency was spending forty hours per quarter per account, manually analyzing what content performed, building briefs, and writing copy. That work needed to happen before anything could be created or scheduled. Task: I needed to build a system that could perform that analysis and generate the briefs automatically, with output quality high enough that the team could use the results with minimal editing. Action: I designed and built a pipeline that integrates with the Pinterest API to pull quarterly analytics, processes the data in BigQuery to identify high-opportunity content categories, and calls the Gemini Pro API with a structured prompt grounded in brand guidelines to generate the content briefs. I built an evaluation layer to validate output quality before delivery and a feedback loop to improve prompt quality using historical performance data. Result: the system reduced quarterly planning time from forty hours to under twenty minutes per account, with generated brief quality rated acceptable or above by the team in eighty-seven percent of first-pass outputs. The agency was able to scale from managing twelve accounts to twenty-eight with the same planning headcount.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
