## Session goal

Explain how AI content generation works to a client with no technical background.

## Shadowing passage

> When I explain the AI content generation system to a client, I start with the outcome, not the technology. I ask: have you ever noticed that after posting content on Pinterest for a while, you develop an intuition for what works — what topics get saves, what visuals drive clicks? The AI has built that same kind of pattern recognition from analyzing your data. It does not write content from scratch. It learns what has performed best for your audience, what content categories are underserved relative to that performance, and what writing style fits your brand voice. Then it generates briefs that match those patterns. The technical reality is that this involves a language model, semantic search through your brand guidelines, and a validation step — but those are implementation details. What matters to the client is: the output reflects their specific performance history, not generic AI-generated content. The quality bar matters: I show them the evaluation process — every brief is scored against three criteria before delivery. And I always set an honest expectation about automation limits: the system gets you eighty to ninety percent of the way. The strategist's judgment for edge cases, new product launches, or creative pivots is still where human expertise adds the most value.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
