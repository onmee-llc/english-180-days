## How to use this module

For each question:
1. **First pass:** Answer out loud with notes allowed.
2. **Second pass:** Answer out loud — no notes, no preparation.
3. **Record yourself** on at least one question per session and listen back.

**Target:** Each answer should feel automatic — natural, structured, confident.

## Scoring yourself

After each answer, ask:
- Was the structure clear? (Situation → Task → Action → Result)
- Did I give at least one concrete number or metric?
- Did I focus on what I did, not just what the team did?
- Did the answer take 2–4 minutes?

## Question 1 — Tell me about a challenging project you led from start to finish

**What the interviewer expects:** Full STAR story. Technical complexity, your ownership, specific decisions, measurable results. They want to see you can lead, not just contribute.

**Structure your answer:** Set the challenge (S) → Your role and scope (T) → Key technical decisions and steps (A) → Quantified outcome (R).

## Question 2 — Describe a time you had to make a technical decision with incomplete information

**What the interviewer expects:** How you handle ambiguity. What information you gathered, what assumptions you made, how you de-risked, and whether the decision worked out.

**Structure your answer:** The ambiguous situation (S) → The decision you needed to make (T) → Your analysis process and the choice (A) → Outcome and what you learned (R).

## Question 3 — Tell me about a time you built something under time pressure

**What the interviewer expects:** How you prioritize, cut scope intelligently, and still deliver quality. Show discipline under pressure, not chaos.

**Structure your answer:** The deadline and why it mattered (S) → What you needed to deliver (T) → How you prioritized and what you cut (A) → What you delivered and the trade-offs (R).

## Question 4 — Describe a project where you had to learn a new technology quickly

**What the interviewer expects:** Learning agility. How you ramp up: documentation, prototyping, asking the right people. Show you're not afraid of the unknown.

**Structure your answer:** The new technology and why it was needed (S) → Your ramp-up responsibility (T) → How you learned it (A) → What you built and how quickly (R).

## Question 5 — Tell me about a time your approach didn't work and what you did

**What the interviewer expects:** Honest failure, not a disguised success. What went wrong, how you recognized it, what you changed, what you learned. Maturity and self-awareness.

**Structure your answer:** What you tried and why (S+T) → What went wrong and how you recognized it (A) → What you changed (A continued) → The eventual outcome and lesson (R).
