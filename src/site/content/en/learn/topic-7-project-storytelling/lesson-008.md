## Session goal

Tell your career arc as a story with a clear thesis.

## Shadowing passage

> When I tell my career story, I frame it as a deliberate evolution from execution toward leverage. I started as a software engineer writing backend code for other people's products — building features, shipping APIs, optimizing queries. I was good at execution. The shift came when I joined a startup where I was the only engineer: I had to own product strategy, infrastructure decisions, third-party integrations, and customer communication simultaneously. That experience revealed something: the most valuable work wasn't any single piece of code — it was the architecture decisions that enabled ten engineers to build independently, or the infrastructure that made deployment safe enough to ship many times per week. I deliberately built toward systems thinking. I sought out architecture review responsibilities, took on platform ownership, and started building tools that amplified the work of other engineers rather than directly building features. The third phase of my arc is now: building systems that amplify entire workflows, not just individual engineers. The AI automation platform I built is an expression of that. One person, building a system that does work that previously required a team. The thesis of my arc is: from individual execution, to engineering leverage, to system leverage. Each transition required deliberately stepping out of what I was already good at.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
