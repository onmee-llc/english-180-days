## Session goal

Tell the complete story of your best project — polished, timed, end-to-end.

## Shadowing passage

> The content automation platform is the project I'm most proud of technically. Let me give you the complete picture. Background: I was consulting with Pinterest content agencies managing multi-million-dollar Pinterest ad budgets. Quarterly content planning consumed thirty to forty analyst hours per account — manually pulling analytics, identifying trends, building briefs, writing copy. At ten accounts, that was four hundred hours per quarter — a full-time person's work on a single process. Problem clarity: the bottleneck wasn't creativity — it was the analytical layer before creativity. Which content categories are underperforming relative to their opportunity? What does the brand guideline actually say about tone? What content format is trending for this audience segment? All of this was being done manually and inconsistently. The build: I built a pipeline that pulls and processes analytics in BigQuery, retrieves brand context with RAG against the guideline document, and generates structured content briefs using Gemini Pro with chain-of-thought prompting. An evaluation layer scores every output before delivery. The performance loop embeds past output scores alongside the generated content in the vector store, so future retrievals bias toward high-performing patterns. Results: planning time from forty hours to under twenty minutes per account. Output acceptance rate — briefs rated usable without major editing — at eighty-seven percent. System uptime: ninety-nine point four percent over twelve months. The agency scaled from twelve to twenty-eight accounts in the following year without adding headcount to the planning function. The technical achievement I'm most proud of is the evaluation loop — it's the piece that makes the system improve over time rather than stay static.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Time yourself: could you tell that story in 5 minutes flat, without notes?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Interview Mode *(5 min)*

Someone just asked you the Reflection question in a **real interview**. Answer now.

- You have **60 seconds**. No pauses longer than 3 seconds.
- Fill gaps with: *"Let me think about that…"* / *"The way I see it…"* / *"To give you a concrete example…"*
- **Record this answer** separately from your shadowing recording.
- Play it back. Did you sound like someone they'd hire?

> Goal: Simulate interview pressure. The ability to speak under pressure without translating is what separates "good English" from "interview-ready English".
