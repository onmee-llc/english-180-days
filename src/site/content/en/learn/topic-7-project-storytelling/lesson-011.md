## Session goal

Set up a compensation conversation with the context of your technical impact.

## Shadowing passage

> Compensation conversations go better when you've established your technical value in concrete terms before any number is spoken. The sequence matters. First, during the interview process, you tell specific, quantified impact stories: the system I built processes twenty-eight accounts' quarterly planning in twelve minutes, down from forty hours. That number does its own work. Second, you understand the full compensation structure before you respond to any offer: base salary, equity, bonus structure, benefits, remote work policy, engineering budget for tooling. You cannot evaluate a number without the denominator. Third, when asked for your number, I give a range anchored to research, with justification: 'Based on my research of compensation for senior backend engineers in this market with AI pipeline experience, I'm targeting a base in the range of X to Y. Within that range, I'd weight toward X if the equity component is structured as Z.' The anchor is research and market data, not what I earned before. What I earned before is irrelevant to my value in the new role. The justification makes the range a reasoned position, not an arbitrary number. If the initial offer is below my range, I respond with data, not emotion: 'I appreciate the offer. Based on the scope of the role and my research, I was expecting something in the range of X. Is there flexibility there?'

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Role-play: the interviewer asks 'what's your salary expectation?' Respond out loud, timed.
