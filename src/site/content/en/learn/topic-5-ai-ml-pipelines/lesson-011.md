## Session goal

Articulate the product value of AI in your content automation SaaS.

## Shadowing passage

> The mistake most engineers make when building AI products is describing the technology instead of the value. When I talk about the AI features in my content automation SaaS, I don't say 'we use Gemini Pro with RAG over brand guidelines.' I say: our system learns what content performs best for your specific audience and creates briefs that reflect those patterns — without your team spending six hours on quarterly planning. The AI is invisible; the value is the outcome. I've internalized three principles for building AI products. First: reduce the activation energy for a pattern users already have. The AI doesn't introduce a new behavior — it makes the existing behavior faster and higher quality. My customers were already doing content planning manually. The AI does it better, not differently. Second: build trust before autonomy. Start with AI-assisted workflows where humans review outputs. Earn trust through quality and predictability. Add automation gradually as users become confident in the system. Third: design for when the AI is wrong. AI outputs will occasionally be wrong. The product must make it easy to identify and correct errors, and errors must never feel catastrophic — they should be minor inconveniences with a clear correction path. The systems that win with AI are the ones where wrong is recoverable.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Anti-Translation Drill — Think in English *(5 min)*

Pick **one key phrase** from today's table. Explain what it means using **only English**.

- No Vietnamese. No dictionary.
- If you don't know a word, describe it: *"It's when you…"* / *"It's similar to…"* / *"The opposite would be…"*
- Then use the phrase in a new sentence about your own experience.

> Goal: Train circumlocution — the skill of explaining anything without knowing the exact word. This is what fluent speakers actually do.
