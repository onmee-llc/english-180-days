## Session goal

Practice owning the entire offer conversation, not just the base salary.

## Shadowing passage

> The non-salary terms are often easier to negotiate than salary, and engineers rarely ask. Here is how to handle a few specific ones. Start date: 'My current notice period is four weeks. I could potentially compress to three if needed, but I want to make sure I leave my current team in a good position. Is a four-week start date workable for you?' Equipment: 'Do you have a standard equipment policy for remote engineers? I currently use a MacBook Pro with an external monitor setup — would the company be able to cover that, or is there a stipend for home office setup?' Remote flexibility: 'I noticed the job description says remote-first. Are there any in-person commitments — quarterly offsites, for example — or is the role fully distributed? I want to make sure my expectations are accurate before I sign.' The meta-skill here is this: treat everything in the offer letter as negotiable until it is explicitly stated that it is not. The worst they can say is no. And every term you improve is real value over the course of a year.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
