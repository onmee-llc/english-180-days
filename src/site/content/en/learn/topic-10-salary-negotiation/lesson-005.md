## Session goal

Practice mentioning a competing offer in a way that is honest, warm, and effective.

## Shadowing passage

> You have an offer from Company A for five thousand two hundred dollars per month. You are interviewing with Company B and you want the role there, but you need them to move. Here is how to mention the competing offer. 'I want to be transparent with you — I am currently evaluating another offer. The role is similar in scope and the compensation is slightly higher. I want to be clear that Company B is my first preference — the mission and the engineering challenges here are more aligned with where I want to invest my career. I am raising this only because I have a deadline on the other offer of Friday, and I was hoping we could get to a decision by then. Is there any way to accelerate the process or match the compensation?' Notice: I did not name the other company. I did not demand a match. I gave them a reason to move faster that is about my constraint, not a threat. And I was explicit that they are my first choice — which removes the adversarial framing and makes it easier for them to say yes.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Do you currently have a competing offer? If not, how would you handle the conversation without one?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
