## Session goal

Compare contracting rates to employment salaries intelligently and articulate the difference.

## Shadowing passage

> If a company offers me a contracting arrangement, the conversation shifts significantly. A full-time employee at five thousand dollars per month has employer-paid benefits, paid leave, and employment security. A contractor at five thousand dollars per month effectively earns less — they pay their own taxes, fund their own leave, and absorb their own downtime. My rule of thumb for comparing contractor rate to employee salary: multiply the employee monthly salary by 1.4 to get the equivalent contractor rate. So a five-thousand employee salary corresponds to roughly seven thousand dollars per month as a contractor. When I discuss contracting terms, I am explicit about this. 'I want to make sure we are comparing the right numbers here. As a contractor, I will be responsible for my own taxes, insurance, and leave — which means the equivalent rate to a five-thousand employee salary would be around six thousand eight hundred to seven thousand per month. Is that in range for the budget you have for this engagement?' This is not aggressive — it is economically accurate. Most clients respect that you understand the difference.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Calculate your desirable monthly contractor rate now. Is it high enough to cover taxes and leave?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
