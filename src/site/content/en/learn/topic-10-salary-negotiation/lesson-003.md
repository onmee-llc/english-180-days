## Session goal

Practice the exact words of a salary counteroffer — firm, warm, reasoned.

## Shadowing passage

> The offer is five thousand dollars per month. You want five thousand five hundred. Here is the negotiation conversation. 'Thank you for putting together this offer — I am very excited about the role and the team. I have given it careful thought over the past two days. The offer is close to what I had in mind, but based on my research on market rates for senior engineers with production AI integration experience, and considering the value I believe I can bring to this specific problem you are working on, I was hoping we could get to five thousand five hundred per month. Is there any flexibility on the base?' Then stop talking. Silence is your ally. The most common mistake is to keep talking after you have named your number — to fill the silence with justifications or concessions. Make your ask, name your number, stop. If they say there is no flexibility: 'I understand. Are there other aspects of the package — like an earlier performance review at six months, or an additional week of PTO — that might be adjustable?' This expands the negotiation surface beyond base salary without sounding greedy.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Say your counter offer number out loud right now. Does it sound confident or apologetic?


## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
