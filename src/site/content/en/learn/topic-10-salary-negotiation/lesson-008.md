## Session goal

Lock in an early review commitment before signing — this is the best time to ask.

## Shadowing passage

> The moment of offer acceptance is the highest-leverage moment in your entire employment relationship. You have all the leverage you will ever have. Use it. Here is a specific thing to ask at acceptance time. 'I am very excited to accept this offer. I want to be transparent about one thing: the base is slightly below what I was targeting, but I am comfortable moving forward given the role and the team. In order to commit fully, I would like to ask that we schedule a formal performance and compensation review at the six-month mark — not the standard twelve months — with a clear agreement that if my performance exceeds expectations, that is reflected in the compensation at that review. Is that something we can agree to in writing?' You are not asking for a raise. You are asking for a fair evaluation sooner. Most reasonable employers will agree. And you have now created a documented commitment and a concrete milestone. This is far more effective than hoping you will be noticed and rewarded.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
