## Shadowing passage

> Let me walk through a complete negotiation scenario from first call to acceptance. They've made an offer: $185,000 base, 200 shares vesting over four years with a one-year cliff, $10,000 signing bonus. I want to negotiate. First response — never accept on the first call: 'Thank you — I'm genuinely excited about this role and the team. I'd like to take 24 hours to review the full package before responding.' This is professional and buys time to prepare. My counter: I've done my market research. Comparable roles at your stage in this market are between $200,000 and $215,000 base. I'm currently at $195,000 and was hoping to see meaningful progression in this move. I'm not going to lowball them — I'll ask for $210,000. On equity, 200 shares at the last round price is approximately $120,000 over four years, or $30,000 per year. For a staff-level role, I'd expect this closer to $50,000 per year — I'll ask for 350 shares. For signing: I'm leaving $40,000 in unvested RSUs. I'd like to see the signing bonus cover at least half — I'll ask for $30,000. Their counter: they meet on base at $205,000, hold on equity at 220 shares, and raise signing to $20,000. I accept. Total improved value over four years compared to the first offer: approximately $85,000. The negotiation took one conversation and one email.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.
