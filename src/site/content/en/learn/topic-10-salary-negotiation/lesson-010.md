## Session goal

Practice the conversation of asking for a raise: data-first, business-case framing.

## Shadowing passage

> Asking for a raise from a current employer is different from negotiating an offer. Here the relationship exists, the history is known, and the emotional stakes are higher. My approach: prepare a one-page document, not a spreadsheet. Sections: what I have delivered this year — specific results with numbers; what the market rate is for my role and skills — specific sources; what I am asking for — specific number; and what my commitment is in exchange. Then I request a meeting and frame it correctly. 'I would like to have a conversation about my compensation — I have some data I want to share with you and I want to understand the company's perspective.' In the meeting: lead with value delivered, not time served. Do not say 'I have been here two years.' Say 'In the last twelve months I shipped the AI pipeline that reduced content cost by forty percent and handled the on-call response that recovered the payment system in eleven minutes.' Then present your market data. Then make your ask. Then stop talking. The most dangerous thing you can do is make your ask and then immediately start qualifying or apologizing for it.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Write down the three most specific things you have shipped in the last 12 months. Those are your raise case.
