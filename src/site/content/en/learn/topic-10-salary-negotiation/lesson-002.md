## Session goal

Buy yourself evaluation time while keeping the offer warm and the employer engaged.

## Shadowing passage

> They call you with an offer: five thousand dollars per month, fully remote, thirty days paid leave. Here is how I respond. 'Thank you so much. I am genuinely excited about this opportunity and the team — the conversations I have had throughout the process have confirmed that this is the kind of engineering environment I am looking for. I want to make sure I give this the careful consideration it deserves. Could I have until this Friday to review everything and come back to you with a response or any questions? I do not anticipate any issues, but I want to be thorough.' Notice: I expressed genuine enthusiasm — because if I am going to negotiate, I need the employer to believe I am interested. I bought two to three days without giving a reason that sounds like I am shopping for a counteroffer. And I gave a specific day, not 'a few days' — that signals I am organized and I respect their timeline. Do not accept immediately even if you like the offer. You almost always have a few days. Use them.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

How long do you have to evaluate an offer before it expires? Practice asking for time gracefully.


## Anti-Translation Drill — Own Words *(5 min)*

Retell today's shadowing passage in **your own words** — do NOT repeat the original phrasing.

- Paraphrase the entire content as if explaining to a colleague who missed the meeting.
- Speak for **60–90 seconds**.
- Use different sentence structures, different transitions, different examples if possible.
- If you catch yourself quoting the passage word-for-word, stop and rephrase.

> Goal: Full integration — you own the knowledge when you can express it freely, not just repeat it.
