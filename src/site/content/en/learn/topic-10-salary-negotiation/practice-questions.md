## How to use this module

For each question:
1. **First pass:** Answer out loud with notes allowed.
2. **Second pass:** Answer out loud — no notes, no preparation.
3. **Record yourself** on at least one question per session and listen back.

**Target:** Each answer should sound confident, not desperate. Professional, not aggressive.

## Scoring yourself

After each answer, ask:
- Did I anchor on my value before stating the number?
- Did I sound confident without being arrogant?
- Did I leave room for negotiation?
- Did I keep it collaborative, not adversarial?

---

## Question 1 — What is your salary expectation?

**What the interviewer expects:** A clear answer with a range. Not "I'm flexible" or "What's the budget?" They want to know if you're in the right ballpark.

**Structure your answer:** Value anchor (experience + skills) → Range (bottom = actual minimum) → Open to full package discussion → Turn it back: "What does the structure look like?"

## Question 2 — We'd like to offer you $4,500/month. What do you think?

**What the interviewer expects:** A thoughtful response, not immediate acceptance. They may have room to move.

**Structure your answer:** Express gratitude and interest → Buy time ("Could I have a couple of days?") OR Counter immediately with evidence → Frame as collaborative.

## Question 3 — We can't go higher than what we offered. Is that a dealbreaker?

**What the interviewer expects:** Mature handling. Explore non-salary items (equity, review timeline, remote allowance, signing bonus). Don't bluff unless you'll walk.

**Structure your answer:** Acknowledge → Pivot to other package elements → Ask about performance review timeline → Make your decision based on total value.

## Question 4 — Why should we pay you more than our initial offer?

**What the interviewer expects:** Evidence. Market data, specific skills (AI/LLM integration), relevant experience, competitive offers. Not entitlement.

**Structure your answer:** Specific value you bring (AI + backend is rare) → Market data for this role → Speed to impact (no ramp-up needed) → Restate the number confidently.

## Question 5 — Do you have other offers?

**What the interviewer expects:** Honesty. Having other offers increases your leverage, but don't fabricate. If you do have offers, mention them without threatening.

**Structure your answer:** If yes: "I do have another offer I'm evaluating, but this role is my preference because [specific reason]. I want to make sure the package works so I can commit fully." If no: "I'm in active conversations with a few companies, but I'm most excited about this opportunity."
