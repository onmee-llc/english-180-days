## Shadowing passage

> Most engineers focus only on base salary. Let me walk through a complete compensation negotiation. The full package has five components: base salary, equity, signing bonus, benefits, and title. Base salary is the most visible but not always the most negotiable in large companies with rigid salary bands. Equity is often more flexible. If they say the base is fixed, ask: 'Is there flexibility on the equity component, given the base is at the band ceiling?' If they make a stock grant offer, ask about the vesting schedule — four years with a one-year cliff is standard — and the strike price if it's options. Ask for the last independent company valuation. Signing bonuses compensate for unvested equity you're leaving behind. If you're leaving unvested stock, calculate its value at the vesting schedule and present it: 'I'm leaving $60,000 in unvested RSUs that vest over the next 18 months. To make that whole, I'd need a signing bonus of at least $45,000.' Framing it as a compensation-for-leaving calculation is more effective than just asking for more. Remote allowance: if fully remote, ask for a home office setup budget — $2,000 to $5,000 is common. Some companies offer annual stipends. Title is often negotiable when base isn't. Moving from Senior to Staff Engineer is worth more than a small salary bump long-term because it changes your next offer baseline.
