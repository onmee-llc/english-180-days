## Session goal

Write sections 1 and 2 of your article draft. Do not edit while writing — complete the sections first.

## Shadowing passage — write fast, edit later

> First draft writing has one rule: do not stop to fix anything. If a word is wrong, write the wrong word and keep going. If a sentence is awkward, let it be awkward. The goal of a first draft is to get the argument out of your head and onto the page in any form. You cannot edit a blank page, but you can always improve a rough draft. Many writers, especially non-native writers, spend too long on individual sentences during the first draft. This is the wrong order of operations. Get the full argument on the page. Then edit. The difference between a published article and an abandoned draft is usually not quality — it is completion. Articles that exist, even imperfectly, have value. Articles that live in a writer's head, waiting for perfection, have none.
