## Session goal

Write sections 3 and 4 — typically the failure and the solution. These are the most valuable sections of the article.

## Shadowing passage — the failure story

> The middle of a technical article is where most writers lose confidence. It is easy to write a polished introduction. It is easy to write a confident conclusion. The middle is where the real details live — the specific error message, the metric that didn't move, the configuration change that finally worked. This is also where many writers become vague. They write "we had some issues with performance" instead of "at peak load, our p95 latency was 8 seconds." They write "after several attempts, we found a solution" instead of naming the specific change. Vagueness is a failure of trust. It tells the reader: I don't trust you with the full story, or I'm not sure this is worth sharing. Specific details do the opposite. A specific failure mode, with a specific metric, shows that this really happened. The reader can learn from it because it is real, not a parable.
