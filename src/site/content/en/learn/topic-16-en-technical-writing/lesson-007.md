## Session goal

Finish the first draft. By the end of this session, all sections including the conclusion must exist.

## Shadowing passage — completing the draft

> The conclusion of a technical article is not a summary — it is a return. You opened with a specific problem. The conclusion returns to that problem and shows what changed. If the introduction asked: "What do you do when your LLM system starts hallucinating pricing data in production?" — the conclusion answers that question in the simplest possible English. One paragraph. Two if necessary. The conclusion also contains the forward-looking sentence: where this leads, what you're trying next, or what you recommend to the reader who is facing the same situation today. The last sentence of the article matters more than any other sentence except the first. Most readers skim to the conclusion before deciding whether to read the middle. Write the last sentence with the same care you gave the opening hook.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the shadowing passage.
**Minutes 6–10** — Shadow the passage.
**Minutes 10–25** — Write remaining sections + conclusion.
**Minutes 25–30** — Read the FULL draft once from start to finish, without editing.

## Anti-Translation Drill

Summarize the entire article in 90 seconds. Include: the problem, the failure, the solution, and the lesson. English only.
