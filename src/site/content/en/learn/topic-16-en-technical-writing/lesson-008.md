## Shadowing passage — reading your own writing

> There is a technique that every editor recommends and almost no one uses: read your draft out loud. Not to yourself silently — out loud, at speaking pace, as if you are presenting it at a meeting. The awkward sentences will reveal themselves. The places where you run out of breath are the places where the sentence is too long. The places where your voice drops are the places where the writing is weak. The places where you read a sentence and think "wait, what?" are the places where the logic broke down. Silent reading is too forgiving. Your brain automatically corrects minor errors and fills in missing logic. Your voice cannot. When something sounds wrong when spoken, it reads wrong too — the reader just can't hear it.
