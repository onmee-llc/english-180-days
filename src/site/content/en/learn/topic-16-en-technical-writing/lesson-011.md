## Session goal

Complete the final editing pass. This is the last substantive change to the article before publishing.

## Shadowing passage — the final editing pass

> After the grammar tools have done their work, one editing pass remains — the human pass. This pass is not about grammar. It is about coherence. Does the article do what the introduction promised? Does each section connect to the one before it? Is there any paragraph that could be removed without the reader noticing? That paragraph should be removed. In good technical writing, nothing is decorative. Every sentence does work. Read the conclusion and then read the introduction. Do they feel like they belong to the same article? If the conclusion introduces a new idea that was not set up in the introduction, move that idea earlier or cut it. A strong final draft has no loose ends and no promises it didn't keep.

---

## Final editing checklist

Work through the draft one last time:

**Structural coherence:**
- [ ] Introduction promise is fulfilled by the conclusion
- [ ] Every section connects to the previous one (add transitions if needed)
- [ ] No paragraph can be deleted without loss — if one can, delete it

**Sentence quality:**
- [ ] No sentence over 25 words (split any that are)
- [ ] Every section begins with a strong topic sentence
- [ ] No filler phrases: "it is worth noting that", "as we can see", "in order to"

**Technical accuracy:**
- [ ] All numbers, metrics, and technical terms are correct
- [ ] Technical terms are defined the first time they appear
- [ ] No claims that you can't back up with your own experience

**Voice check:**
- [ ] Read the draft aloud one final time
- [ ] Does it sound like you? Or like a generic AI article?
