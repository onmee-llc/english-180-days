## Shadowing passage — on learning to write in English

> Writing in English for the first time as a working engineer is a specific kind of difficult. You already think clearly in your language. You already know what you want to say. The frustration is not the idea — it is finding the right English sentence to carry it. Every professional who writes in a second language goes through a phase where the English version of their thoughts sounds thinner than the original. Simpler. Less precise. That phase is real, and it is temporary. The solution is not to write less — it is to keep writing until the English becomes capable enough to hold the full thought. This takes time. But every article you write in English expands what the next one can say. After writing one article, the second is easier. After ten, your English writing voice is as precise as your thinking. You are at the beginning of that process now.
