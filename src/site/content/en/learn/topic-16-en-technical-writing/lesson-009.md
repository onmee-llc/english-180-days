## Session goal

Complete Speaking Session 2 — present the article's main finding to a colleague or small group in English.

## Shadowing passage — knowledge sharing

> A knowledge-sharing session is different from a pitch. A pitch is asking someone for their time and attention. A knowledge share is giving someone something they can use. That shift in frame changes how you prepare. Instead of "how do I sound credible?" you ask "what is the most useful thing this person can take from this conversation?" Usually the most useful thing is not the solution — it is the problem definition. If you can articulate the problem clearly enough that the listener immediately recognizes it from their own experience, the solution becomes obvious. Most technical knowledge shares fail because the presenter starts with the solution. Start with the problem. Let the listener experience the recognition moment first. Then the solution lands with weight.

---

## Speaking Session 2 structure (10 minutes)

**Opening (1 minute):**
- "I want to share something I've been working on for about 5 minutes."
- "The problem it addresses is [one sentence]."

**Main content — 3 key points (6 minutes, 2 minutes each):**
- Point 1: The problem — what it looked like in practice
- Point 2: What we tried and what didn't work
- Point 3: What actually worked and why

**Q&A (2 minutes):**
- "Does anyone here have a similar problem in their own work?"
- Answer at least one question in English.

**Close (30 seconds):**
- "The article will be published on [platform]. I'll share the link when it's live."

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the shadowing passage.
**Minutes 6–10** — Shadow the passage.
**Minutes 11–18** — Practice the 10-minute session structure alone, out loud.
**Minutes 19–28** — Deliver the session to a real person (colleague, friend).
**Minutes 29–30** — Post-session reflection.

## Post-session reflection

1. What question did the audience ask that I hadn't prepared for?
2. What phrase did I use that felt natural in English?
3. What vocabulary did I want but couldn't find?
