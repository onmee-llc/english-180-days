## Session goal

Complete Speaking Session 3 — a 15-minute English discussion about one specific technical decision from your KPI 1 or KPI 2 work.

## Shadowing passage — technical discussion in English

> A technical discussion is not a presentation. In a presentation, you control the flow. In a discussion, you don't. The skill is different. You need to listen carefully enough to respond to what was actually said, not to what you expected to be said. You need to disagree with a colleague in English without it feeling like a confrontation. You need to say "I'm not sure" or "I don't have the data for that" in a way that sounds confident, not weak. These are harder skills than presenting a prepared talk, and they are also more valuable. Your colleagues will judge your English fluency most by how you handle the unexpected questions — not by how smoothly you deliver a rehearsed opening.

---

## Speaking Session 3 structure (15 minutes)

**Setup (2 minutes):**
- "I want to discuss a specific decision we made in [KPI 1 / KPI 2] and get your thoughts."
- State the decision in one sentence.
- State why it was difficult — "The trade-off was between X and Y."

**Discussion (10 minutes):**
- Explain your reasoning. Use data if available.
- Invite disagreement: "Do you think this was the right call? What would you have done differently?"
- Respond to the feedback in English. Don't concede just because it's easier than defending in English.

**Close (3 minutes):**
- "Based on this conversation, my view is still [or: I've updated my view to] _____."
- "The one thing I'd like to think more about is _____."

## Phrases for navigating difficult discussions in English

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| I need time to think | "That's a good point. Let me think about it before I answer." |
| I disagree | "I see it differently. My concern with that approach is _____." |
| I don't have the data | "I don't have the numbers in front of me, but my sense is _____." |
| I agree with a caveat | "That's true, with one qualification — _____." |
| I want to move on | "Let's put a pin in that and come back to it." |
