## Week 1 (Days 197–203): From blank page to first draft

**Writing target:** Complete a full first draft — rough, unedited, but complete.

**Skills focus:**
- Write every day without stopping to self-edit — first draft speed is more important than quality
- Use the outline as a guide, not a rigid structure — adjust sections if the writing reveals a better structure
- Prioritize completing sections over polishing individual sentences

**Weekly checkpoint:**
- [ ] Article topic chosen and scope narrowed (Lesson 1)
- [ ] Outline written — 5–7 sections with one-sentence purpose for each (Lesson 2)
- [ ] Introduction written (Lesson 3)
- [ ] Speaking Session 1 completed (Lesson 4)
- [ ] Sections 1–4 written (Lessons 5–6)
- [ ] Conclusion written (Lesson 7)
- [ ] Full first draft complete by Day 203

## Week 2 (Days 204–210): Edit, publish, and speak

**Writing target:** Publish the final article on Dev.to and LinkedIn.

**Skills focus:**
- Edit for clarity, not length — don't cut content, cut complexity
- Use Hemingway Editor and one AI grammar review — do not over-rely on tools
- Speaking sessions: be honest about your writing process and what you found difficult

**Weekly checkpoint:**
- [ ] Speaking Session 2 completed (Lesson 9)
- [ ] Grammar + AI review complete (Lesson 10)
- [ ] Final human editing pass done (Lesson 11)
- [ ] Speaking Session 3 completed (Lesson 12)
- [ ] Article published on Dev.to (Lesson 13)
- [ ] Article cross-posted to LinkedIn (Lesson 13)
- [ ] Speaking Session 4 completed (Lesson 14)
