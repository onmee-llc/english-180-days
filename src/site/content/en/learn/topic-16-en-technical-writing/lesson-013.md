## Session goal

Publish the article on Dev.to. Cross-post to LinkedIn. Share with one colleague.

## Shadowing passage — the moment before publishing

> There is a feeling most writers know well: the draft is ready, the editing is done, and still you do not press publish. Something feels unfinished. The article could still be improved. The conclusion isn't quite right. There is always one more change. This feeling is not a signal that the article needs more work. It is the feeling of exposure — the difference between writing that lives in a document only you can see and writing that exists in the world. That feeling does not go away when the article is good enough. It goes away only when you press publish anyway. The good-enough article that ships is worth more than the perfect article that never does. Every piece of writing you publish teaches you something that editing in private cannot.

---

## Publishing steps — Dev.to

1. Go to [dev.to](https://dev.to/) → Sign in → Write a Post
2. Paste the final draft
3. Add tags: `llm`, `machinelearning`, `python` or relevant to your topic
4. Add a cover image (optional — simple gradient or diagram from your article)
5. Preview and read once more
6. Click **Publish**

## Publishing steps — LinkedIn cross-post

1. Go to LinkedIn → Write an article (not a post)
2. Paste the article content
3. Add the note at the top: "Originally published on Dev.to: [link]"
4. Publish
5. Also post a short LinkedIn post (250–400 words) summarizing the article with the link

## LinkedIn post template

> I published a new technical article: **[Article title]**.
>
> It covers [2-sentence description of the problem and what you found].
>
> The key insight: [one sentence].
>
> If you're working with [relevant technology or problem space], it might be useful.
>
> [Dev.to link]
