## Session goal

Complete Speaking Session 1 — pitch your article to a colleague, engineer, or friend in English.

## Shadowing passage — pitching in English

> The hardest moment in a technical speaking session is the opening. After the opening, language flows more naturally. So I prepare my first sentence exactly. I don't memorize the whole pitch. I memorize the first sentence and the last sentence. The first sentence puts me in motion. The last sentence gives me a landing spot when I'm ready to stop. Everything in between can vary. The goal of this session is not to have a perfect conversation — it is to have a real one. Real questions, genuine answers, and at least one moment where I had to find words for something I hadn't prepared. That improvisation is the practice. A rehearsed pitch in an empty room is preparation. A conversation with a real person who asks an unexpected question — that is the session.

---

## Speaking Session 1 structure (5 minutes)

**Your pitch (2–3 minutes):**
- "I'm writing a technical article about [topic]."
- "The problem I'm describing is [one sentence]."
- "My main finding or recommendation is [one sentence]."
- "I'm planning to publish on [Dev.to / LinkedIn] in [timeframe]."

**Q&A (2 minutes minimum):**
- Invite questions: "Does this match anything you've run into?"
- Answer at least one question in English before the session ends.

**Closing (30 seconds):**
- "The main thing I want readers to take away is [one sentence]."

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the shadowing passage.
**Minutes 6–12** — Shadow the passage.
**Minutes 13–20** — Practice the pitch alone — aloud. Time it.
**Minutes 20–28** — Deliver the 5-minute pitch to a real person (colleague, friend, anyone).
**Minutes 29–30** — Write 2 sentences about what happened: what you found difficult and what worked.

## Post-session reflection
