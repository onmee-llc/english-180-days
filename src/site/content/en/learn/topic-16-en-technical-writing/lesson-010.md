## Session goal

Complete the AI-assisted grammar and clarity pass. Fix marked issues from Day 204. Preserve your voice.

## Shadowing passage — using AI tools for writing

> There is a right way and a wrong way to use AI writing tools. The wrong way is to paste your draft and ask: "Make this sound better." The result will be generic, polished, and completely not you. No one publishes AI-polished writing under their own name and feels good about it. The right way is more surgical. You paste a single paragraph and ask: "Check this for grammar and naturalness. Do not rewrite it — only flag what is wrong." You read the suggestions. You accept only the ones that are clearly correct errors — missing articles, wrong verb tense, a preposition used in an unusual way. You reject anything that changes the meaning or replaces your word with a more formal-sounding synonym. Then you open Hemingway Editor and paste the draft. You fix only the red sentences — the ones it marks as very hard to read. You leave the orange ones if they feel right. The goal of this pass is to remove errors, not to make the writing sound like an essay.

---

## Step-by-step AI grammar review

**Step 1 — Prepare the prompt:**
```text
Review the paragraph below for grammar, clarity, and naturalness.
Do not rewrite it — only flag specific issues and suggest minimal corrections.
Preserve my voice and my original sentence structure.

[Paste your paragraph]
```

**Step 2 — Review by section:**
Do one section at a time. Review the suggestions before moving to the next section.

**Step 3 — Hemingway Editor:**
Go to [hemingwayapp.com](https://hemingwayapp.com/) and paste the full draft.
- Fix ALL red sentences (very hard to read)
- Review orange sentences — fix only if you agree they're unclear
- Target: Grade 9 or below

**Step 4 — Apply fixes manually:**
Go back to your draft and apply the changes yourself. Do not let AI rewrite sections.
