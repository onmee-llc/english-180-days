## Questions for every writing session

Ask yourself these questions before starting to write:

1. Who is the reader? A junior developer? A technical manager? A peer with the same background?
2. What do I want the reader to do or know after reading this?
3. What is the one main claim? (If you can't state it in one sentence, you're not ready to write.)

---

## Questions for self-editing a draft

After completing a section, ask:

1. Is every sentence 25 words or under? If not, split the long ones.
2. Did I use any Vietnamese-influenced sentence structures? ("Because of that" for "therefore", compound-subject constructions)
3. Is every claim backed by a concrete example or data point?
4. Did I start any sentence with "I think" or "In my opinion"? Delete those — just state the claim.
5. Is every technical term defined the first time it appears?
6. Does each paragraph have one clear topic sentence?
7. Does the conclusion repeat the opening claim but in different words?

---

## Questions for speaking session preparation

Before each speaking session:

1. Can I summarize the article (or topic) in 30 seconds?
2. What is the most controversial or surprising claim I'm making?
3. What question am I most afraid someone will ask? (Prepare the answer in English.)
4. What is my call to action — what do I want the audience to do after?
5. What will I do if someone asks a question I can't answer in English?
   > "That's a great question. Let me think about it and get back to you." — acceptable
   > Switching to Vietnamese — not acceptable in an English session

---

## Article quality self-assessment

Before publishing, rate your article:

| Criterion | Self-rating |
|-----------|------------|
| Clear single main claim | 1–5 |
| Concrete examples and data | 1–5 |
| Active voice throughout | 1–5 |
| No grammar errors | 1–5 |
| My voice — not AI-rewritten | 1–5 |
| Appropriate length (600–1200 words) | 1–5 |
| Engaging title | 1–5 |
| **Total** | /35 |

Publish when total ≥ 28/35.

---

## Speaking session self-assessment

After each speaking session, rate yourself:

| Criterion | Self-rating |
|-----------|------------|
| Opened clearly (no "uh, so, well…") | 1–5 |
| Stayed on topic | 1–5 |
| No Vietnamese | 1–5 |
| Answered at least one question in English | 1–5 |
| Closed with a clear summary | 1–5 |
| **Total** | /25 |

If total < 18, note what to practice before the next session.
