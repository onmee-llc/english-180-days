## Session goal

Articulate exactly what 'backend AI engineer' means in concrete technical terms.

## Shadowing passage

> When I describe myself as a backend engineer who specializes in AI integration, what I mean specifically is this: I build the infrastructure that makes AI features reliable in production. That means designing the pipeline from input processing through model calls to output validation and delivery — including fallback logic, retry handling, cost monitoring, and latency budgets. Most engineers who work with LLMs focus on the prompt side. My focus is the architecture around it — how do you serve five hundred concurrent users through a reasoning model without your system falling over? How do you cache effectively without losing freshness? How do you evaluate whether your AI output quality is degrading over time? Those are the problems I find genuinely interesting. On the backend side specifically, I've worked extensively with Python microservices, PostgreSQL, Redis, and cloud-native infrastructure on both AWS and GCP. Kubernetes, Terraform, CI/CD pipelines — all of that is familiar territory.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
