## Session goal

Identify 3 key words in your intro and make them land with force.

## Shadowing passage

> One significant difference between a confident speaker and an anxious one is not vocabulary — it is rhythm. Confident speakers vary their pace deliberately. They slow down on important words and accelerate through background information. They pause before delivering a key point, so the listener pays attention. They raise their volume slightly on the phrase they most want remembered. Try this with your introduction right now: find the three most important words in your entire answer. Say them louder, slower, and with a slight pause before them. For example: 'I specialize in building systems that make AI' — pause — 'work in production.' Those three words are your emphasis anchors. Everything before and after them is setup. Record yourself twice: once at normal speed, once with deliberate emphasis. Listen to the difference. Practice until the emphasis version sounds completely natural.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

## Anti-Translation Drill — Listening First *(5 min)*

Close the text. Recall today's shadowing passage from memory only.

1. Say the **main idea** in one English sentence out loud.
2. Say **one specific detail** you remember.
3. If you blank out — do NOT open the text. Describe what you vaguely remember using simple English.

> Goal: Build the habit of processing English without reading — ears and memory only.
