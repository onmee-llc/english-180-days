## Session goal

Answer the strength question with specificity — name it, prove it, land it.

## Shadowing passage

> My biggest strength is the combination of backend engineering depth and practical AI integration — which is still relatively rare. I can architect a system to be genuinely production-ready, and I can also reason clearly about where to incorporate AI in a way that actually improves the product rather than just adding complexity. Beyond technical skills, I'd say my strength is clarity of thinking under ambiguity. When requirements are fuzzy, when there's pressure, when the right answer isn't obvious — that's when I tend to do my best work. I think that comes from years of being on small teams where you don't have the luxury of waiting for someone else to make the decision. I can hold complexity in my head, organize it, and produce something clean that others can build on. That's the quality I'm most consistently proud of across all the projects I've done.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Is your strength concrete and unique to you — or could any engineer say it?
