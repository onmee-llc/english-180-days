## Session goal

Speak one full paragraph from your intro with zero filler words.

## Shadowing passage

> The solution to filler words is not to practice being perfect — it is to practice pausing instead. A pause sounds intentional. An 'um' does not. Try this: wherever you would normally say 'um' or 'like' or 'you know' — just stop. Hold that silence for exactly one second. Then continue. You will feel awkward. The person listening will not. They will think you are choosing your words. Here is the test: record yourself answering 'Tell me about yourself' right now, without any preparation. Play it back and count every filler word. Write the number down. Then record a second time with the explicit goal of pausing instead of filling. Count again. Even reducing from fifteen fillers to eight is real progress in one session. The gap between how you think you sound and how you actually sound is where the improvement happens. Today's entire session is that exercise.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.
