## Session goal

Explain your career path as a deliberate arc, not a list of jobs.

## Shadowing passage

> I started in backend development about twelve years ago, right after university. My first job was at a small startup where I was essentially the only backend engineer — which meant learning to make decisions without much guidance, and living with the consequences. That experience shaped how I think about software: it's a tool for solving a real problem, not an end in itself. After that, I moved to a larger fintech company, which gave me exposure to the challenges that come with scale — distributed systems, strict data consistency requirements, and regulatory compliance. About four years ago I started working seriously with LLMs and AI APIs — initially as side projects, then core professional work. I think the intersection of solid backend engineering and practical AI integration is where the most interesting problems are right now. That's the direction I've been moving in deliberately, and it's where I want to keep investing my career.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Does your story have a clear direction — from where you started to where you're going?
