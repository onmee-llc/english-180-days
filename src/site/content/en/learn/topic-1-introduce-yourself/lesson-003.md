## Session goal

Deliver your strongest achievement story naturally, without reading.

## Shadowing passage

> The project I'm most proud of is a document processing pipeline I built at my last company. We were handling about two million records a month — started as a monolith, and as volume grew, the whole system became a bottleneck. I redesigned it as a distributed pipeline: separate ingestion, transformation, validation, and storage services, connected through a message queue. I chose Kafka for the messaging layer because we needed strong ordering guarantees on the transformation phase. The result was significant — processing time dropped by seventy percent, and we eliminated a full-time operations role that had been manually handling failures. What I'm most pleased with, beyond the technical outcome, was that the design was simple enough for the rest of the team to understand immediately. I didn't need a thirty-page document to explain it. That's the kind of work I'm proudest of — architecture that solves the right problem with the right level of complexity.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

What's the single number in your story that impresses most? Lead with it.


## Anti-Translation Drill — Listening First *(5 min)*

Close the text. Recall today's shadowing passage from memory only.

1. Say the **main idea** in one English sentence out loud.
2. Say **one specific detail** you remember.
3. If you blank out — do NOT open the text. Describe what you vaguely remember using simple English.

> Goal: Build the habit of processing English without reading — ears and memory only.
