## Session goal

Expand naturally when the interviewer probes beyond the initial intro.

## Shadowing passage

> In my current role I'm essentially a principal engineer on a small team — there's me and two other backend engineers, and we own the entire platform architecture. Day to day that means a mix of architecture decisions, code review, mentoring, and still writing a meaningful amount of code myself. The domain is content automation: we build systems that generate, schedule, and publish content programmatically. The most interesting part of my current work is the LLM integration layer — I've built a prompt management system, a streaming response handler, and an evaluation framework we use to A/B test prompt changes. I also handle the DevOps side: our CI/CD pipeline, cloud infrastructure on GCP, and cost optimization for our AI API usage. I'm comfortable being the person who makes the hard call when there's ambiguity. That kind of ownership is something I actively seek.

## 30-minute protocol

**Minutes 1–5** — Read the passage silently twice. Focus on meaning, not pronunciation.

**Minutes 6–15** — Shadow aloud with the text visible. 4 full passes. Match rhythm and stress.

**Minutes 16–22** — Shadow without the text. 3 passes. Accept mistakes — keep moving.

**Minutes 23–28** — Record yourself once. Play it back immediately. Note one phrase to improve.

**Minutes 29–30** — Write the phrase you struggled with. Say it 10 times slowly, then at full speed.

## Reflection

Does your answer show OWNERSHIP — decisions you made, not just tasks you completed?
